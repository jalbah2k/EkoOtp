SmartStart Hubs Community of Practice:

Ujima Project webinar - young parents and the developmental needs of their young children

The Ontario Association of Young Parent Agencies is hosting a webinar to demonstrate how the Ujima Project is improving the outcomes of young, equity-deserving parents(age 29 and under) and their children (0-6).

 

Content: Hear about the core components of the project and how Ujima Project programs and services are impacting young families by increasing access and confidence in navigating systems, increasing awareness and advocacy of needs, improving positive parenting techniques, and supporting timely referral and attachment to specialized children's treatment services. Evaluation findings will be discussed, demonstrating a successful proof-of-concept for Ujima Project's model of access to services for young parents that strives to meet their children's early developmental needs. Please see more about the Ujima Project in its recent Impact Report  and Infographic .

 

When: April 10,11:30am - 1:00pm

 

Registration: https://forms.office.com/r/SVrxcxxHd4 (MS Teams)

 

Not able to attend? The session will be recorded. Register and you will receive a recording link after the session.

 

Presenters: Jonine Adridge, Early Literacy Specialist, Rosalie Hall; Ekua Asabea Blair, President, Ontario Association of Young Parent Agencies; President & CEO, Abiona Centre for Infant and Early Mental Health; Tamar Meyer, Director, Ujima Project, Abiona Centre for Infant and Early Mental Health; Katelyn Mitri, Research and Evaluation Consultant, Taylor Newberry Consulting; Gillian Thompson, Nurse Practitioner, Paediatrics/Young Families Program, SickKids
